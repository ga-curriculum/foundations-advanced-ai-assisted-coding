## Why different models—and why “custom” GPTs?

> 💡 Not all AI models are the same. Some are designed for general conversation, others are optimized for coding, data tasks, or even image analysis.

Let’s break it down:

- **General models** (like GPT-4o, GPT-3o, or GPT-4.1)  can do a bit of everything—writing, answering questions, coding, even images.
- **Specialized models** might be trained just for code, math, or particular industries.
- **Custom GPTs** are “personality” layers you create in ChatGPT, giving you a version of the model with special instructions, rules, and behaviors. They can remember your preferences and follow your guidance.

---

## What can a custom GPT do for you?

> 🧠 With a custom GPT, you can:
>
> - Tell it to always ask clarifying questions before suggesting code.
> - Force it to output *only* pseudocode, not runnable Python.
> - Have it prompt you for missing details in a workflow.
> - Set up reusable conversation starters for repeated tasks.

**Custom GPTs are like having a “coding coach” who never gets tired and always follows your playbook.**

---

## Capabilities—and limits—of custom GPTs

**Strengths:**
- Always follows your instructions and workflow
- Can act as a “thinking partner” for chunking problems
- Fast, flexible, and accessible via web

**Limits:**
- Memory resets between conversations (doesn’t truly “know” your past projects)
- Can’t run code or connect to databases directly (unless you add tools)
- Needs clear, well-written instructions to perform well

> ⚠ **Custom GPTs are as good as your design.** The more specific you are, the better they’ll help you.

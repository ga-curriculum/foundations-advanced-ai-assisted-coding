Now it’s time to put your custom GPT to work!

---

## Challenge: Use your GPT to tackle a real-world problem

Your task:  
**Use your custom GPT to plan and write pseudocode for a realistic data or coding scenario.**

## Example scenarios (choose one):

💡 **Design a workflow for automated data validation and anomaly detection**  
  You’ve just received a monthly sales dataset with thousands of rows from multiple teams. Plan a process to:
    - Detect and report missing or suspicious values (e.g., negative sales, missing customer IDs, impossible dates)
    - Generate an automated report summarizing anomalies by type and severity
    - Recommend next steps for each flagged issue

---

💡 **Outline the pseudocode for a personalized marketing email campaign generator**  
  Create a step-by-step logic for a system that:
    - Segments customers based on purchase history, engagement, and demographics
    - Selects dynamic email content blocks (subject, body, offer) tailored to each segment
    - Schedules and tracks emails, ensuring no duplicates are sent to the same user within a week
    - Logs and reports campaign performance for A/B testing

---

💡 **Plan the data engineering workflow for real-time fraud detection**  
  You are tasked with designing the logic for a streaming pipeline that:
    - Ingests transaction data in real time from multiple sources
    - Applies a sequence of fraud detection rules (velocity checks, geolocation, amount thresholds, customer behavior anomalies)
    - Flags transactions for manual review and triggers automatic account holds for high-risk events
    - Provides dashboards for analysts to monitor and update detection rules dynamically

---

💡 **Map out the pseudocode for end-to-end customer churn prediction**  
  Your company wants to proactively predict customer churn. Build a logic flow that:
    - Collects and cleans user activity, support tickets, and payment history
    - Builds features for machine learning (recent activity, frequency of logins, support ticket types, etc.)
    - Applies a trained ML model to assign churn risk scores
    - Automates outreach (emails, offers, calls) for high-risk customers and logs all actions for follow-up

---

💡 **Create a multi-stage process for automated compliance reporting**  
  For a regulated financial service, outline a workflow that:
    - Aggregates transactions and account updates from several internal systems
    - Validates data against compliance rules (AML, KYC, transaction thresholds)
    - Generates exception reports for compliance team review, with detailed explanations for each flag
    - Schedules and submits required government filings, and tracks their status

---

💡 **Design a cross-team product analytics data pipeline**  
  The company wants to give Product, Marketing, and Support teams access to unified user journey analytics. Pseudocode a workflow that:
    - Integrates data from web, app, CRM, and support ticket systems (each with their own schema)
    - Cleans, standardizes, and joins data across systems (resolving duplicate user IDs, timestamps, event types)
    - Builds custom dashboards for each team with role-based access controls
    - Enables daily refreshes and automatic alerting on key metrics

---

**Tips:**
- Think about each stage as a function or step in your pseudocode.
- Consider user inputs, outputs, edge cases, and error handling.
- Your GPT should help you clarify requirements before jumping into solutions!

---

## Steps

1. Start a new conversation with your custom GPT.
2. Paste in your chosen scenario or prompt.
3. Answer any follow-up questions your GPT asks.
4. Work together to break down the problem and generate detailed pseudocode.
5. If needed, refine your prompt or update your GPT instructions on the fly.


6. (Stretch) Have the GPT write your code then take it into a colab notebook.
    - Use Colab's built in AI features to refactor, edit, or add new functionalities!

## Reflection

- How did your GPT perform?  
- Where did it add the most value?  
- What (if anything) was missing or confusing?
- Would you update your GPT for future tasks?

Share your experience and your best pseudocode with the class!
